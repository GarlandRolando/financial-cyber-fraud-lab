# Regulatory market map

The project direction is relevant across several financial centres:

## United States

Bank supervisors continue to identify cyber threats, fraud, scams, sanctions,
money laundering, and model risk as important banking risks.

## United Kingdom

Financial firms are expanding AI use while regulators emphasise cyber risk,
third-party dependency, operational resilience, governance, and accountability.

## European Union

DORA creates continuing requirements for ICT risk and third-party resilience.
The new AML Authority is building common supervision and data frameworks.

## Singapore

Singapore is expanding responsible AI governance and cyber resilience while
financial institutions continue to invest in fraud and financial crime analytics.

## Hong Kong SAR

The HKMA is expanding AI adoption in risk management and anti-fraud while
strengthening cyber and operational resilience expectations.

## Indonesia

Digital financial crime, scams, suspicious transactions, anti-fraud controls,
and banking technology governance are growing supervisory priorities.
